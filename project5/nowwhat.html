I found a stray dog! Now What???

  1. Ensure the dog's safety

    • If the dog seems approachable and non-aggressive, try to secure the dog by fastening a spare leash or improvised lead (such as a belt or piece of rope) to ensure that the dog doesn’t run into traffic or after other people. Using a leash or improvised lead also keeps you safe as the handler because it puts distance between you and the dog while keeping them secure.

  2. Check for identification!

    • If the dog has a collar or a harness, check to see if there are identification tags. In Syracuse and other municipalities, it is often required for a dog to have ID tags with a point of contact for the original owner. If there are tags, try to contact the owner and meet at a local public place, like a bank parking lot or in front of a police station!
    • If there are no tags, there is still the possibility of getting the dog scanned for a microchip. Microchips are usually located between the dog’s shoulder blades and are encoded with the owner’s point of contact. The microchip can be scanned at a local veterinarian’s office, or at an animal shelter. JUST BECAUSE A DOG HAS NO TAGS DOES NOT MEAN IT HAS NO OWNER!

  3. Contact Animal Control

    • If you are within the city limits of Syracuse, the number for Animal Control is 315-442-5346
    • If you reside in a surrounding community, the number for Onondaga County Animal Control is (315) 442-5346

  4. Transport to a shelter

    • If it is a weekend, or after business hours, you may need to take the dog to a shelter yourself. Refer to our Availability Report of Local Shelters page here.
    • If you don’t have the ability to transport the dog yourself, call one of the listed local shelters and ask for a volunteer to transport the dog

  5. Post the dog on social media!

    • You can find local lost dog pages on Facebook or other community resource pages. Make it clear that the animal has been or will be transported to a local shelter and IS NOT available for sale or private rehoming!
